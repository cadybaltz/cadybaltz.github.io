About Me

Hi! My name is Cady and I am an honors computer science student at the University of Texas at Dallas. I have been coding since middle school, when I was first introduced to HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.


At UT-Dallas, I am an officer for the Association for Computing Machinery, the Society of Women Engineers, and the Artificial Intelligence Society. I am also an Enterprise Technology intern at State Farm in Richardson, TX.


I have experience in web development with HTML/CSS/JavaScript, and have more recently started using TypeScript as well. I have worked with both the React and AngularJS frameworks. I have mobile app development experience with Swift, and I am trying to work more with React Native and Flutter.


I also have backend development experience with a variety of languages and frameworks, including Python, C#, Java, and JavaScript. I have built REST APIs using Node.js/Express.js and Java/Spring Boot. I tend to use Google Firebase as my backend solution for databases and authentication, and am a big fan of the Google Cloud Platform.


My primary interests are in the areas of Natural Language Processing, Extended Reality, and (like every computer science major) Artificial Intelligence. To explore these interests, I have worked on several projects, which you can check out on this site!


Outside of tech, I love learning new languages, traveling, and baking.

Orbit

1st Place - HackUTD VI (SG Challenge)

Orbit is an iOS application that allows UTD students to quickly scan an event's flyer to view the details of the event and add it to their phone's calendar in an effort to increase student engagement with campus activities.

I worked on both the frontend and backend of this app. On the frontend, I coded the page navigation of the app. In the backend, I added the ability for users to automatically save an event they scan to their phone's calendar, using the EventKit framework. I also worked on adding a points system to gamify the app.

This project was created with a team of three other students for HackUTD VI, where we won first place for the Student Government Challenge at HackUTD VI.

VR Assistant

"Mischa" is a virtual reality assistant that organizes tasks, schedules events, and improves mood as determined by sentiment analysis.

I worked on the backend of this application, developing the algorithm that generates conversations between the user and the assistant. To create the dialogue, I created a custom Google Dialogflow agent. I also queried the Google Cloud Natural Language API to perform sentiment analysis on the user's responses, which she then responds to. For example, when a user is upset, Mischa helps the user engage in stress-relieving activities, like guided meditation. When the user is happy, Mischa plays their favorite songs.

I worked with a team of four other students to complete this project, as part of the Fall 2019 ACM Projects program.

Python Flask Dialogflow Google Cloud NLP Selenium Unity C#

SWE UTD Website

In my new role as an officer for the UTD Society of Women Engineers, I was tasked with creating a brand new website for the organization.
